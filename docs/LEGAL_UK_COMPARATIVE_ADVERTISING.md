## What's DEFINITELY allowed

- **Comparative advertising per se is lawful** — BPRs 2008 reg. 4 permits it if the 8 tests are met.
- **Naming a competitor without permission** — permitted under TMA s.11(2)(c) (honest practices) + BPRs reg. 4. Confirmed in *O2 v Hutchison 3G (C-533/06, ECJ)* — still leading authority.
- **Displaying competitor prices side-by-side** — permitted if objective, verifiable, current, comparing like-for-like (BPRs reg. 4(a), CAP 3.35).
- **Subjective "puffery"** ("the best since 2004" — no reasonable consumer takes as objective) — ASA advice.
- **Aggregating public reviews** — protected as intermediary opinion (Trustpilot model). Notice-and-takedown per Defamation Act 2013 s.5.

## What requires CARE (defensible but risky)

- **Superlatives ("cheapest", "fastest", "UK's best")** — ASA treats unqualified superlatives as objective comparative claims against **all** competitors and demands substantiation for the whole universe. Three Mobile's "The UK's Best Value" was read as a hard price claim → upheld against them.
- **"Save £X vs [competitor]"** — Wise's "saved vs banks" ruled misleading in 2025 because consumers read it as actual saving not hypothetical.
- **Feature-tick tables** — Osborne Clarke: generic disclaimers ("check with the provider") do **NOT** cure a misleading overall impression if you can't evidence the competitor's actual capability.
- **"As at [date]" without contemporaneous evidence** — stale comparisons breach reg. 4(a) verifiability. Practitioner rule: keep dated screenshots on file for every claim.
- **Any content that could denigrate** — CAP rule 3.41 (updated 6 Apr 2025) specifically calls out comparisons with identifiable competitors. Sarcasm, mockery, negative implication = denigration even if literally true.
- **Verifiability signposting** — ASA 17 Sep 2025 ruling: the basis + link/reference must appear IN the ad, not on a separate methodology page.

## What is NOT allowed

- **Imitation / replica presentation** of a trade-marked competitor product (BPRs reg. 4). *Thatchers v Aldi [2025] EWCA Civ 5*: visual design that "reminds consumers" of the TM = unfair advantage.
- **Confusing consumers into thinking your product IS the competitor's** — BPRs reg. 4(b), now cross-referencing DMCCA s.226. Penalty: CMA direct fine up to **10% of global annual turnover**.
- **Discrediting / denigrating** competitor TMs, names, products, activities (BPRs reg. 4). *Wild Pack / Montdog* (16 Apr 2025 ASA): claiming vets were bribed = denigration.
- **Comparing products that don't meet the same need/purpose** — CAP 3.34. Don't compare a full platform against one feature of a competitor.
- **Taking unfair advantage of a competitor's reputation** — *Thatchers v Aldi [2025]*. "Like [X], only cheaper" phrasing is legally hazardous.
- **False factual statements made with malice** — malicious falsehood tort. Post-*George v Cannell [2024] UKSC 19*, likelihood of financial loss at time of publication is enough for nominal damages; substantial damages if actual loss shown. **No serious-harm threshold for business claimants** — much lower bar than defamation.

## DMCCA 2024 — the big shift (in force 6 April 2025)

**Part 4 Chapter 1** — replaces CPRs 2008 + amends BPRs reg. 4.

- **s.226 (Misleading actions)** — a practice is misleading if the info OR its overall presentation is likely to deceive the average consumer AND causes/likely causes a transactional decision. **Explicitly includes true information presented misleadingly** — this is new and it bites hard.
- **s.227 (Misleading omissions)** — omitting "material information" the average consumer needs. Includes failing to identify commercial intent (affiliate/referral fees).
- **Penalty regime**: CMA can now determine breach **itself** (no court) and impose fines up to **10% of worldwide annual turnover**. Court-based route also up to 10% turnover. Previously CMA had to sue.
- **New banned practices**: fake/misleading reviews (own OR competitor), drip pricing.

## Recent case law + ASA rulings 2024-2026

| Case / ruling | Date | Lesson |
|---|---|---|
| *Thatchers v Aldi [2025] EWCA Civ 5* | Jan 2025 | Lookalike packaging took unfair advantage of TM even without literal copying. Internal benchmarking docs used as evidence of intent. |
| *Viovet* [2024] EWHC 777 | 2024 | "Save £X per day" / "Swap and Save" implied comparable nature/quality — implicit comparisons trigger BPRs. |
| *George v Cannell* [2024] UKSC 19 | 2024 | Malicious falsehood — likelihood of loss at publication creates presumption of nominal damages. **No serious-harm threshold**. |
| *Wild Pack / Montdog* (ASA) | 16 Apr 2025 | Negative claims about a category of competitors = denigration if members identifiable. |
| *Tesco Clubcard v Nectar* (ASA) | 12 Feb 2025 | Well-drafted disclaimer that scopes what you're NOT comparing = defensible pattern. |
| *Octopus Energy* (ASA) | 23 Apr 2025 | Savings claim misleading — didn't disclose it only applied to switchers from non-Octopus SVTs. Audience-for-whom-true must be spelled out. |
| *Wise "saved vs banks"* | 2025 | Misleading because context read as actual saving not hypothetical. |

## Safe-harbour patterns Trustpilot / G2 / Which? use

1. **UGC / third-party opinion model** — reduces malicious falsehood + defamation exposure.
2. **Methodology page linked from every chart.**
3. **"As at [date]" stamp on every table.**
4. **Fixed rubric rating** — objective + material + verifiable + representative.
5. **Show BOTH strengths of each competitor** — defeats s.227 misleading-omission risk.
6. **No visual mimicry** of competitor brand (post-Thatchers, no palette/typography borrowing).
7. **Cite competitor's own public pages** for every feature-tick — you're reporting their claim.
8. **Full TM attribution footer.**
9. **Response mechanism**: "Competitor: think this is wrong? Contact us" — reduces malice inference.
10. **Never adjectival denigration** — pure facts, neutral tone. No "clunky", "outdated", "overpriced".
11. **Disclose commercial relationships** (affiliate links, referral fees, own-product prominence) — mandatory under DMCCA s.227.
12. **Written correction protocol** (14-day review commitment) — evidence of "honest practices" under TMA s.11(2)(c).

## Cease-and-desist risk profile

1. **Solicitor's letter before action** (LBA) — usually 7-14 day takedown demand.
2. **Unjustified threats caveat** — TMA has this provision, shaky C&Ds are themselves actionable.
3. **Interim injunction application** — urgent, discretionary; American Cyanamid test.
4. **Full trial** — damages or account of profits (elective, not both).
5. **ASA complaint** — free, weeks not months, no damages but public ruling forces takedown.
6. **CMA complaint (post-DMCCA)** — up to 10% global turnover fine, direct CMA enforcement.

**Practical damages exposure for a small SaaS**: interim injunction defence typically £50k-£150k in legal fees. Final trial £250k-£1M+. Most disputes settle after LBA.

## What our current comparison chart does right

Already applied on `/trade-off/compare-platforms` (as of 2026-07-18):
- Text names only, no logos, no visual mimicry (Thatchers-defence)
- 17 objective boolean feature dimensions (BPRs reg. 4(a))
- "As at [Month Year]" stamp — prominently bolded in-chart
- Methodology anchor + link in-chart (ASA verifiability standard)
- Fair-picture rows: 2 categories where competitors beat us (s.227 defence)
- Rescoped superlatives — "on these N dimensions", "of the N platforms we surveyed"
- Trade mark attribution footer citing TMA s.11(2)(c) correctly
- 14-day correction contact — `admin@thenetworkers.app`
- Honest disclosure panel — explicit "where competitors beat us"
- Commercial intent disclosed
- Regulatory framing citing BPRs 2008 reg. 4 + DMCCA ss.226-227 + CAP Code

## Before public publish — checklist

- [ ] **Evidence file per competitor** — screenshot public pages, save dated to `docs/comparison-evidence/{competitor}/`. Required for BPRs reg. 4(a) verifiability defence + malicious falsehood defence.
- [ ] **UK IP/advertising solicitor review** — 30 mins, £200-500 (LawBite, Farrer, any commercial IP firm). Have them review the exact wording.
- [ ] **Quarterly re-verification calendar entry** — every 3 months, re-check every competitor row.
- [ ] **Correction mailbox monitored** — `admin@thenetworkers.app` must be watched for competitor complaints. 14-day SLA committed publicly.
- [ ] **Legal hold on scraped competitor pages** — if a competitor changes their page after you scored them, you need proof it was true when you scored it.
